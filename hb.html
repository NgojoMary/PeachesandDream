Heart Beats
My Lord, don’t exit through that door.
Don’t leave me,
And become a city light.
I won’t allow you.
Stay with me.
Just don’t become
A boy,
Who suffocated me
With promises that were never made,
With affection that were only said and
With the reeds of his heart and called it love.

A boy,
Who said that every heart beat,
Was another memory
But just like heart beats
You forget them easily,
You forget they’re even there.
And you were amazing at forgetting
Truly a magic trick of yours
You were good at that, too.
Tricking people, casting spells,
And always left as a mystery of how you did it.
Always left like a puzzle with a missing piece,
Unsolved.
And I, was one that fell for your mystic.
But anyway this all just a heart beat,
For you.
Just one of those you would forget to remember
When it is still there,
Just a heart beat,
That follows every night,
Staring outside trying to count the lights
Along with the lies you say and
Along with the reasons you couldn’t stay
And with every heart beat follows a memory
That I’ll slowly try to forget, too.
